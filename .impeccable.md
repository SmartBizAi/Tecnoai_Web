# TecnoAI Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Miami small business owners — pharmacy managers checking orders on mobile, restaurant owners managing operations at their desk, field team coordinators, inventory managers. They interact daily: real operations, real pressure, real stakes. They are working people who need tools that respect their time and don't require training.

### Brand Personality
Smart, accessible, Miami-fast. The interface should feel like the most capable person in the room who also happens to be easy to talk to. Confidence without arrogance. Clarity without condescension. Speed without chaos.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Light primary, dark available.** Light for daytime operations (most use cases), dark for evening work and reduced eye strain. Both must feel premium, not like a theme toggle afterthought.

**Visual tone**: Stripe's precision + Shopify's warmth + Notion's flexibility. Clean, purposeful, no decoration for decoration's sake. Navy (#0f2250) as the brand anchor — trustworthy, serious, Miami-professional.

**References**: Stripe (simplicity, trust), Shopify (approachability, everyday business use), Notion (flexibility, feels built for people)
**Anti-references**: Dark gray enterprise tools, overcomplicated SaaS dashboards, anything that looks like it requires a training session

### Design Principles
1. **Every element earns its place** — if it doesn't help the user accomplish their goal, remove it
2. **Speed is a feature** — interactions must feel instantaneous; animations serve comprehension, not decoration
3. **Accessible to everyone** — small business owners are not necessarily technical; clarity always beats cleverness
4. **Brand consistency across languages** — EN/ES parity is a product requirement, not an afterthought
5. **Flagship quality** — this is what investors and franchise partners see first; every screen is a proof point

### Typography
- Display/headings: **Epilogue** — geometric, editorial confidence, distinctive without being loud
- Body/UI: **Figtree** — warm, clean, highly readable, accessible for non-technical users

### Color Anchors
- Brand navy: `brand-900` (#0f2250) — primary brand, logo, key CTAs
- Brand blue: `#2563eb` (CSS `--brand`) — accent, emphasis, interactive
- Never use gradient text (banned pattern)
- Tint neutrals subtly toward brand hue
